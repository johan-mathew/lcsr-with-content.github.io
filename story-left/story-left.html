LCSR Success Stories: Right to Internet now a Fundamental Right.

The Kerala High Court recently made headlines by declaring Right to Internet access as part of Right to Privacy and Right to Education. This verdict came on a petition filed by Faheema Shirin, a student of Sree Narayanaguru College, Chelannur who challenged her expulsion from the college hostel for not adhering to the restriction imposed on mobile usage.

She was evicted from her hostel for refusing to abide by the arbitrary rule imposed by the hostel authorities wherein the inmates of the girl’s hostel were not allowed to access their mobile phones within the hostel premises between 6pm – 10pm. This illogical restriction which is not only discriminatory as it was only imposed on the girl’s hostel also impaired the quality of education accessible to the female students. It is her right to acquire knowledge through internet and by prohibiting the use of mobile phone, she is deprived of the access to the source of knowledge to her detriment which will affect the quality of her education. In addition, forceful seizure of mobile phones is an invasion of the right to privacy of the hostel inmates. Aggrieved by the action of the Authorities, she filed a Writ Petition before the Hon’ble High Court of Kerala seeking redress.

Mobile phones which were considered to be a luxury has now become a part and parcel of the day to day life such that it is unavoidable to survive with dignity and freedom. On availability of more and more facilities, like access to e-platforms, e-books, various applications etc, the dependence on such gadgets has increased tremendously over the period. It is a known fact that there is a large scale misuse of modern technologies like internet, mobile phones etc but that shouldn’t result in restrictions that prevent innumerable advantages to the public on its proper use. The court observed that a student above the age of 18 years shall be given the freedom to choose the mode for her studies provided it does not cause any disturbance to others. When one student may be interested in garnering knowledge by reference of books in libraries, one may be interested in referring to e-books or downloading data.

The court added that the hostel authorities were expected to enforce only those rules and regulations for enforcing discipline. Enforcement of discipline shall not be by blocking the ways and means of the students to acquire knowledge. The rules should be modified in tune with the modernisation of technology. The court further said that college authorities as well as parents should be conscious of the fact that the students in a college hostel are adults capable of taking decisions as to how and when they have to study. The total restriction on its use and the direction to surrender it during the study hours is absolutely unwarranted. Justice Asha observed “When the Human Rights Council of the United Nations have found that right to access to Internet is a fundamental freedom and a tool to ensure right to education, a rule or instruction which impairs the said right of the students cannot be permitted to stand in the eye of law.”

It is for each of the students to decide with self-confidence and self-determination that they would not misuse it and that they would use it only for improving their quality of education. It is to be understood that enforcement of discipline shall not be by blocking the ways and means of the students to acquire knowledge. This judgement is indeed a breakthrough with respect to the rights of students and the team from LCSR is more than happy to provide assistance to all the aggrieved students just like Faheema Shirin.
